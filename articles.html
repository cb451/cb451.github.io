Want to Help Professors Become Better Teachers? Find Them a Mentor / Beth McMurtrie

Highlights the importance of mentorship among teachers, and how new or less experienced faculty can improve their methods by connecting to knowledgeable teachers.

Beth McMurtrie."Want to Help Professors Become Better Teachers? Find Them a Mentor." The Education Chronicle. June 28, 2018. Web. 22 Jul 2018.

tags: pedagogy, teachers, college


Why My MOOC is Not Built on Video / Lorena Barba

Barba's short article discusses the use of video in learning and how material that is seemingly absorbed through that medium can be lost shortly after. This hints at the need for hands-on enagement with material to promote interiorization.

Barba, Lorena. "Why My MOOC is Not Built on Video." Class Central. March 5, 2015. Web. 22 Jul 2018.

tags: learning methods, personal knowledge, students


Study finds popular 'growth mindset' educational interventions aren't very effective / Michigan State University

Discusses a MSU meta-analysis based study that seemed to show that growth mindset interventions did not lead to academic improvement. States that the some of the practices used to base these claims may have been flawed.

Michigan State University. "Study finds popular 'growth mindset' educational interventions aren't very effective." ScienceDaily. ScienceDaily, 22 May 2018.

tags: pedagogy, learning methods, students, high school

Linking Students’ Emotions and Academic Achievement: When and Why Emotions Matter / Carlos Valiente, Jodi Swanson, and Nancy Eisenberg

“This article briefly reviews findings linking situational and dispositional negative or positive emotions to academic achievement and suggests that researchers can learn much about relations between emotions and achievement by considering the potential moderating role of effortful control, as well as considering the mediating roles that cognitive processes, motivational mechanisms, and classroom relationships play in linking emotions and achievement.”

tags:


Just stop thinking about it": effects of emotional disengagement on children's memory for educational material / Rice JA, Levine LJ, Pizarro DA.

“The authors examined the effects of emotional disengagement and engagement on children’s memory for educational material.” Results suggest that intentional emotional disengagement leads to better memory recall than “working through one’s feelings” for the mild affect produced during the experiment.

tags:


Racial identity and academic achievement in the neighborhood context: a multilevel analysis / Byrd C, Chavous T.

 “Using hierarchical linear modeling, the authors examined the relationship of racial identity to academic outcomes, taking into account neighborhood-level factors .[…] in neighborhoods low in economic opportunity, high pride was associated with a higher GPA, but in more advantaged neighborhoods, high pride was associated with a lower GPA. The authors discuss the need to take youth’s contexts into account in order to understand how racial identity is active in the lives of African American youth.”
