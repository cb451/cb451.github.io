Papers

Tagged and annotated bibliography of academic papers.

Tacit Knowledge in Stories of Expert Teachers / Jana Krátká

Discusses the division of knowledge into "narrative" and "academic," "tacit" and "explicit." Proposes teacher narratives as methods of transferring personal knowledge about the craft to beginner colleagues.

Jana Krátká. "Tacit Knowledge in Stories of Expert Teachers." Procedia - Social and Behavioral Sciences. 171(2015): 837 – 846. Print.

tags: pedagogy, personal knowledge, teachers


Sleep promotes branch-specific formation of dendritic spines after learning / Guang Yang et al

Documentation of a study that highlights the importance of sleep in dendrite spine formation. Explains that “reduction in spine formation could not be compensated for by subsequent sleep” following periods of sleep deprivation after learning.

Guang Yang et al. "Sleep promotes branch-specific formation of dendritic spines after learning." ScienceMag. 344.6188(2015): 1173-8. Web.

tags: neuroscience, students


Learning How We Learn / Barbara Oakley

Discusses compartmentalization and how it inhibits learning, and points to the benefit of shared interdisciplinary knowledge as an approach to understanding how we learn.

Oakley, Barbara. “Learning How We Learn.” IEEE Pulse, 13 Mar 2015, pp. 37-41.

tags: neuroscience, learning methods, pedagogy


Dynamic network centrality summarizes learning in the human brain / A. V. Mantzaris et al

Summarizes a study that suggests that measured brain activity becomes more efficient and therefore decreases as a task is learned, and that “the brain might require less communication between distributed functional networks as skills become more automatic.”

A. V. Mantzaris et al. “Dynamic network centrality summarizes learning in the human brain.”

tags: neuroscience, learning methods


The TEA Set: Tacit Knowledge and Scientific Networks / H. M. Collins

Discusses the "diffusion network" of knowledge in the scientific community and the informal, tacit nature of a large part of the knowledge that comprises it.

tags: personal knowledge


Active learning increases student performance in science, engineering, and mathematics
/ Scott Freeman et al

Presents a meta-analysis pointing to the ability of "active learning" (a broad term for learning that invovles class activites or discussion) to notably improve student performance.

Scott Freeman et al. "Active learning increases student performance in science, engineering, and mathematics." PNAS, 111.23(2014): 8410-8415. Web.

tags: pedagogy, learning methods, students, college


A discussion focusing on Polanyi’s “Tacit Knowing”
/ Justin Fruehauf et al

Provides a summary of Polanyi's thoughts on tacit knowledge and attempts to contextualize his tenents based on "the contemporaneous and historical circumstances" that "made up his environment."

Justin Fruehauf et al. "A discussion focusing on Polanyi’s “Tacit Knowing”" Online Journal of Applied Knowledge Management, 3.2(2014): 100-113. Web.

tags: personal knowledge
